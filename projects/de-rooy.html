Intelligent Pickup Prediction System

A machine learning solution for logistics optimisation

Unexpected pickups make up over 82% of De Rooy's daily assignments. I built a multi-model pipeline that forecasts where, when, and how large those pickups will be, wired into a Streamlit dashboard that planners use to cut distance, cost, and CO2.

01 / overview

Working with De Rooy Transport, I designed a predictive system for the challenge of unexpected pickups. It uses a multi-model machine learning pipeline to forecast pickup details with high accuracy and integrates with the PTV route-planning software the planners already use.

02 / the models

Each part of the prediction is handled by the model best suited to it:

  • Pickup occurrence: a Keras sequential model over 5 km grid cells, reaching 90.2% accuracy, 88.9% precision, and an 86.5% F1-score.
  • Exact location: a random forest classifier with a mean distance error of 18 metres and 100% of predictions within 1 km.
  • Size and quantity: random forest regressors estimating loading metres and shipment units for route planning.
  • Insertion time: an XGBoost regressor that brought the mean error down to about one hour, improving schedule integration.
  • Feature engineering: spatial, temporal, and holiday features that made the models more robust during peak periods.

03 / impact

Sample tests in May 2025 showed savings of several hundred kilometres per day, with matching reductions in fuel cost, CO2, and planning time. The system was designed to roll into daily routes from August 2025, with potential daily savings of up to 1,500 km and a clear path to expand across the wider Benelux network.
