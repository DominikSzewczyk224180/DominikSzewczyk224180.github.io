// Antea Group and Kavel 10 · 2024 to 2025

AI-Powered 3D Digital Twin and Object Removal

Automating 3D urban modelling with AI

A research project into AI methods for reconstructing urban environments from aerial and drone imagery. We combined semantic segmentation, generative object removal, and Neural Radiance Fields to improve 3D mesh quality for urban planning.

01 / overview

Our team researched how to automate the reconstruction of urban environments. The work involved custom machine learning pipelines for object removal, semantic segmentation to detect and isolate structures, and NeRF for high-fidelity 3D visualisation, all aimed at improving 3D meshes built from aerial imagery.

02 / image inpainting and segmentation
  • Tested and compared DeepFill v2, LaMa, and PowerPaint for object removal on client data.
  • Built a custom pipeline with a CLI so users can preprocess data, adjust model parameters, and train segmentation models.
  • Trained a semantic segmentation model to detect and isolate trees, ready for AI-powered object removal.
  • Evaluated U-Net, DeepLabV3, and FCN on accuracy and IoU, selecting FCN as the most effective.
03 / 3D reconstruction and NeRF
  • Used Instant NeRF to generate 3D visualisations from short drone video sequences.
  • Created, refined, and optimised point clouds in NeRF Studio for 3D mesh generation.
  • Cleaned point clouds and produced detailed meshes with improved visual fidelity.
  • Built a VR environment in Unreal Engine for immersive exploration of the reconstructed areas.
04 / deliverables

The project ended with reports on data quality, project lifecycle, and model recommendations, including a NeRF implementation guide, plus a CLI for data preparation and training. We presented the findings to mentors, peers, and the client at a final conference.
