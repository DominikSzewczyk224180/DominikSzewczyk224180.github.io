Plant Analysis with Computer Vision and Robotics

Computer vision and robotics in synergy

A project with NPEC on plant phenotyping, combining computer vision for root segmentation with a simulated robot for precise liquid handling, tuned with reinforcement learning.

01 / overview

My focus was advancing plant root segmentation from images and developing a robotic system for precise liquid handling, to improve phenotyping and automate the process.

02 / what I did
  • Applied computer vision to isolate the Petri dish and run semantic segmentation on plant images.
  • Built a model to predict masks for seeds, shoots, and roots, with instance segmentation for individual plants and root-tip localisation.
  • Simulated a precision liquid-handling robot with a PID controller for accurate dispensing.
  • Used reinforcement learning, with reward shaping and hyperparameter tuning, to optimise the robot's navigation and delivery, tracked with ClearML.
